1. The Homepage: The "Nashville Market Authority" Hub
   The homepage is the most powerful page on your site. Its goal is to rank for the most competitive "money" keywords in Middle Tennessee.

Target: High-intent, broad search terms (e.g., "Pool Removal Nashville" or "Pool Demolition Tennessee").

Role: It acts as the central hub that receives the "cumulative authority" from all sub-pages. It must reference "Limestone," "Metro Stormwater," and "Belle Meade" to anchor the domain geographically and technically.

2. Service Pages: The "Geotechnical Expertise" Builders
   The goal of these pages is to prove to Google that you are a subject matter expert in the specific physical challenges of the Nashville basin.

Target: Specific entities/sub-topics (e.g., "Limestone Rock Excavation," "Certified Structural Backfill," or "Stormwater Runoff Compliance").

Role: They provide a "Density of Authority." By having a page for the 'hard rock' challenges in West Nashville, you demonstrate deep domain knowledge that generic competitors lack.

Instruction: Do not keyword stuff. Instead, explain the geology of the Cumberland Plateau or the mechanics of vibratory soil compaction in high-clay environments.

3. Location Pages: The "Contextual" Anchors
   For Nashville, these pages show Google the specific high-value neighborhoods we dominate.

Target: Specific luxury enclaves (e.g., "Belle Meade," "Green Hills," "Forest Hills," or "Brentwood").

Role: They signal to Google's knowledge graph that your business is physically and contextually relevant to the specific high-value areas you want to rank in.

Instruction: Talk about specific service problems unique to that location (e.g., why East Nashville's narrow lots require specialized tight-access machinery vs. the large estates of Forest Hills).

4. "Zero Search Volume" Pages: The "Authority Density" Secret
   Some pages target technical terms that tools say have zero search volume but are essential for authority.

Goal: To show Google's algorithm that you haven't skipped any regulatory or technical details.

Role: Pages like "Metro Nashville Stormwater Permit Guide" or "Proctor Density Testing for Nashville Clay" help the entire domain rank better by increasing its "Authority Density" score.

Summary of the "Network" Goal:
By linking all these highly related entities (Geology + Local Regulations + Neighborhoods) back to your homepage, you prove you are the most relevant and authoritative result for the Nashville market.
